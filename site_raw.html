Display Technology Options

Comprehensive analysis of holographic display approaches, from recommended to experimental:

A. Pepper‚Äôs Ghost (Projection Pit) ‚Äî Recommended

  • Bright continuous image from a hidden screen
  • Real blacks, no visible pixel grid ‚Äî superior image quality
  • Needs depth, masking, and single sweet viewing zone ‚Äî architectural requirements
  • Not walk-in unless we add a second foil (messy, low contrast)
  • Best for: Permanent installations with space and lighting control

B. Transparent LED ‚ÄúHologram‚Äù Panels (AVLink, etc.)

  • Thin, hangable, survives brighter rooms ‚Äî environmental flexibility
  • LED lattice and gray ‚Äúoff‚Äù state visible at 6‚Äì8 ft viewing distance

  • Best for: Backup technology or bright ambient environments

  • Big scale, cheap, fast rig ‚Äî rapid deployment option
  • Looks foggy without hard light control ‚Äî blacks go milky
  • Softer edges than PG ‚Äî less crisp image quality
  • Best for: Temporary installations or budget-constrained projects

D. Boxed ‚ÄúHolo‚Äù Cabinets (Proto/Portl Style)

  • Turnkey, self-contained ‚Äî minimal site preparation
  • Reads like fancy TV in a box up close; shallow depth illusion
  • Good satellite piece, not the main Oracle ‚Äî secondary installations
  • Best for: Touring demos or quick deployment while main build happens

  • Novel, noisy, low pixel density ‚Äî limited practical application
  • Safety issues, can‚Äôt deliver crisp 8 ft face ‚Äî not viable for this scale
  • Best for: Small novelty displays only

  • Seven-figure cost and content headaches ‚Äî not practical for public installation
